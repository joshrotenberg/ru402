Vector similarity

From the above diagram, we can intuitively guess that vectors that are similar represent people with a similar physique (weight+height). How do we define "similarity", though? There are many ways to calculate how similar two vectors are, and this is expressed through different distance metrics.

One distance metric that's easy to visualize in a two-dimensional space is cosine similarity, which relies on the angle between two vectors: the smaller the angle is, the more similar the vectors are, and vice versa: the bigger the angle, the more dissimilar the vectors.

In the image below, we can see that the vectors for U1 and U3 have a small angle between them, which means the two people have a more similar height and weight. U1 and U2, however, have a bigger angle between their vectors, which means their height and weight are not very close.
